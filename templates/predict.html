Prediction Methodology

This system analyzes customer behavior patterns to estimate the likelihood of churn. The prediction is currently based on a rule-based analytical model that simulates real-world business logic used in customer retention strategies.

Factor Impact on Churn Reasoning
Monthly Charges High → Higher Risk Customers with higher monthly costs are more likely to discontinue services due to financial burden or perceived low value.
Contract Type Monthly → Higher Risk Short-term contracts provide flexibility, making it easier for customers to leave without long-term commitment.
Yearly Contract Lower Risk Long-term commitments indicate customer trust and reduce the probability of churn.
Prediction Logic Combined Factors If a customer has high monthly charges and a short-term contract, the system flags them as "Likely to Churn".

Note: This is a foundational predictive approach. In advanced implementations, machine learning models such as Logistic Regression or Decision Trees are used to improve accuracy and capture complex behavioral patterns.
